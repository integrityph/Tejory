FAQ

Find answers to common questions

  • Why should I use a hardware wallet?
  • What is a hardware wallet?
  • How to transfer crypto to a hardware wallet?

Using a hardware wallet is essential for securely storing your cryptocurrency, as it protects your private keys offline, making them immune to online hacking attempts and malware. Unlike software wallets that are connected to the internet, hardware wallets reduce the risk of your assets being stolen through phishing attacks, data breaches, or other cyber threats. They offer enhanced security by requiring physical confirmation on the device for transactions, ensuring that only you can authorize transfers. If you're holding cryptocurrency long-term or have significant amounts, a hardware wallet provides peace of mind, knowing your assets are safe from potential threats in the digital world.

A hardware wallet is a physical device used to securely store cryptocurrency offline, offering protection from online threats and hacking. It stores the user's private keys—essential for accessing and managing crypto—on the device itself, rather than on a computer or mobile phone. Since it operates without being constantly connected to the internet, it significantly reduces the risk of cyberattacks. To make a transaction, users must physically confirm it on the device, adding an extra layer of security. Hardware wallets are ideal for long-term storage of digital assets, ensuring they remain safe and protected from unauthorized access.

To transfer cryptocurrency to a hardware wallet, first set up your device by installing its companion app (e.g., Ledger Live or Trezor Suite) and securing it with a PIN and recovery phrase. Connect the hardware wallet to your computer or mobile device, then generate a public receiving address through the wallet software. Copy this address and go to the exchange or software wallet where your crypto is stored, initiate a transfer by pasting the address, entering the amount, and confirming the transaction. After confirming on your hardware wallet, wait for the transaction to process and verify the transfer through the wallet's app. This ensures your crypto is securely stored offline.
